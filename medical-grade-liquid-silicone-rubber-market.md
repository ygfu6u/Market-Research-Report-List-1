<p><p>The Medical-Grade Liquid Silicone Rubber (LGS) market is categorized into three exposure types: Limited Exposure, Prolonged Exposure, and Permanent Contact. Limited Exposure refers to materials that have short-term contact with body tissues, such as surgical masks or temporary implants. Prolonged Exposure involves longer-duration applications, like catheters or silicone tubing that remain in the body for days or weeks. Permanent Contact pertains to silicone used in devices intended for continuous use, such as implants or prosthetics, where long-term biocompatibility is crucial.</p></p>
