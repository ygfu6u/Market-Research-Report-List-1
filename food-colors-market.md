<p><p>The food colors market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural food colors are derived from plants, minerals, and other organic sources, appealing to health-conscious consumers seeking cleaner labels. They often possess added nutritional benefits but can have variability in color shades and stability. On the other hand, synthetic food colors are chemically manufactured to provide vibrant, consistent colors and longer shelf life, making them popular in the processed food industry, despite concerns about health effects.</p></p>
