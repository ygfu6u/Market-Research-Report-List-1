<p><p>The passenger vehicle aluminum alloy wheel market comprises three main types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into a mold, allowing for intricate designs and mass production. Forging, on the other hand, uses intense pressure to shape aluminum, resulting in wheels that are typically stronger and lighter. Other methods include rolling and extruding, which offer different advantages in terms of cost and performance. Each type serves diverse market needs based on vehicle requirements and consumer preferences.</p></p>
