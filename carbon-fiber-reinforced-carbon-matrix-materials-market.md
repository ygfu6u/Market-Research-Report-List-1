<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Carbon Matrix (CFRCM) materials are advanced composites primarily used in high-performance applications, such as aerospace and automotive sectors. The market for CFRCM is characterized by two main types of production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing a carbon-rich gas onto a substrate, leading to a high-density matrix with exceptional thermal and mechanical properties. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves saturating carbon fibers with a liquid resin that subsequently hardens, offering enhanced flexibility and manufacturability.</p></p>
