<p><p>The calcium chloride food grade market is primarily divided into two types: dihydrate and anhydrous. Dihydrate calcium chloride contains two water molecules and is commonly used as a firming agent in food processing, enhancing texture and preserving moisture. Anhydrous calcium chloride, on the other hand, is water-free and is utilized for its strong hygroscopic properties, making it ideal for moisture control in food preservation and packaging. Both types play vital roles in enhancing food quality and safety.</p></p>
