<p><p>SPECT (Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography) scanning devices utilize various isotopes to produce diagnostic images of the body. Iodine-123 and Thallium-201 are commonly used for cardiac imaging, as they help assess heart function and detect abnormalities. Technetium-99m, the most widely used, provides excellent resolution for a range of conditions, including cancer and bone disorders. Xenon-133 is utilized primarily for pulmonary studies, measuring lung function and blood flow. Fluorine-18 is mainly used in PET scans but can complement SPECT in hybrid imaging approaches. Each isotope serves a distinct purpose, enhancing diagnostic capabilities.</p></p>
