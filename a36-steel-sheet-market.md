<p><p>The A36 steel sheet market is broadly divided into hot-rolled and cold-rolled types. Hot-rolled A36 steel sheets are produced at high temperatures, resulting in reduced residual stresses and improved ductility, making them ideal for structural applications. Conversely, cold-rolled A36 sheets undergo processing at room temperature, delivering tighter tolerances, smoother surfaces, and enhanced strength. While hot-rolled sheets are suited for welded structures, cold-rolled variants are preferred for applications requiring precise specifications and finish quality, such as automotive and appliance manufacturing.</p></p>
