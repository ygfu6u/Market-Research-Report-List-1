<p><p>The Sapphire (Al2O3) crystal market comprises primarily two types: Skarn and Hydrothermal sapphires. Skarn type sapphires are formed through metamorphic processes, often found in limestone environments where mineral-rich fluids interact with host rocks, resulting in unique colors and patterns. In contrast, Hydrothermal sapphires are produced by high-temperature solutions in volcanic environments, offering consistent quality and clarity. Both types cater to different segments of the gemstone and industrial markets, driven by their unique formation processes and properties.</p></p>
