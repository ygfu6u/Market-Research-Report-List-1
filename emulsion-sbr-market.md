<p><strong>The Emulsion SBR Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Cold E-SBR</li><li>Hot E-SBR</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Emulsion Styrene-Butadiene Rubber (E-SBR) market is categorized into Cold E-SBR and Hot E-SBR based on polymerization temperature. Cold E-SBR is produced at lower temperatures (around 5°C), resulting in a polymer with better mechanical properties, higher tensile strength, and improved abrasion resistance, making it ideal for high-performance tires and industrial applications. Hot E-SBR, polymerized at higher temperatures (around 50°C), offers faster production rates and lower costs but with slightly inferior properties, commonly used in general-purpose tires and non-critical rubber goods. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
