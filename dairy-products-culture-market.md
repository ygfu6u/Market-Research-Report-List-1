<p><p>Dairy products culture markets can be categorized into three main types: Mesophilic, Thermophilic, and Probiotics. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, aiding in cheese and yogurt production, while Thermophilic cultures require higher temperatures and are commonly used in yogurt and certain cheeses. Probiotic cultures contain beneficial bacteria that promote gut health and are increasingly popular in functional foods. Each type plays a crucial role in the fermentation process, impacting flavor, texture, and nutritional value of dairy products.</p></p>
