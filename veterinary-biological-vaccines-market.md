<p><p>The veterinary biological vaccines market comprises several types, primarily including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and other vaccine types. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, eliciting strong immune responses without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines, on the other hand, use killed pathogens, providing safe immunity but often requiring booster doses. Other vaccine types may include subunit, recombinant, or DNA vaccines, each designed to target specific diseases in animals, enhancing overall health and preventing outbreaks in livestock and pets.</p></p>
