<p><p>Wire wound RF inductors are categorized into low frequency and high frequency types based on their operational ranges. Low frequency RF inductors typically operate below a few megahertz, suited for applications like power electronics and audio devices, where larger inductance values are required. High frequency RF inductors, on the other hand, function in the megahertz to gigahertz range, making them essential for telecommunications, RF amplifiers, and wireless communication, where compact size and minimized losses are crucial.</p></p>
