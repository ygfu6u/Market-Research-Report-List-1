<p><p>Metal forming in the automotive market encompasses various techniques to shape and manipulate metal components for vehicle manufacturing. Roll forming involves continuously bending metal sheets into desired profiles. Stretch forming stretches metal over a mold, creating complex shapes. Stamping uses presses to cut and shape parts, while deep drawing forms deep, hollow components through a die. Hydroforming utilizes high-pressure fluids to mold metal into intricate shapes. Other methods may include forging and extrusion, catering to diverse automotive design requirements and performance standards.</p></p>
