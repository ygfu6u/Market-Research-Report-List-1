<p><p>The synthetic ink resins market consists of various types, each serving specific applications. Modified rosin resins offer adhesion and gloss, making them suitable for flexographic and gravure inks. Hydrocarbon resins provide excellent solubility and color retention, ideal for high-performance inks. Acrylic resins deliver durability and chemical resistance, enhancing outdoor and industrial applications. Polyamide resins exhibit strong adhesive properties for coatings and adhesives, while polyurethane resins are known for flexibility and toughness, catering to specialty inks and coatings.</p></p>
