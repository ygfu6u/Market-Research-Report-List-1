<p><p>The car tire market primarily consists of radial and bias tires. Radial tires feature layers of fabric or steel cords arranged perpendicular to the direction of travel, offering better fuel efficiency, durability, and grip, making them dominant in modern vehicles. Bias tires have crisscrossed ply layers, providing robust sidewalls and load-bearing capacity, often used in off-road or heavy-duty applications. While radial tires dominate the market due to performance advantages, bias tires remain relevant in specific niches requiring ruggedness and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
